Florian Luca: Concatenating perfect powers

Abstract. Assume that b > 1 is an integer and that A1, ... , At are nonnegative integers written in base b with A1 > 0. We write for the number whose base b expansion is obtained concatenating A1, ... , At.

In my talk, I will look at those instances when one concatenates two nth powers and obtains an nth power. For example, in base 10, the concatenation of the perfect squares 4 and 9 yields 49 = 72 which is again a perfect square, but there do not exist two nonzero cubes whose concatenation is a perfect cube. I shall discuss various diophantine aspects of the problem.

This is joint work with W.D. Banks.

Seminar on Number Theory and Algebra, October 6, 2004.
